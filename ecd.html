Exam Cheating Detection

Eye tracking is considered one of the best methods to detect exam cheating. When people are moving, lighting changes, or the face is partially or fully covered, eye tracking algorithms use to recognize and identify the human face, eyes, iris, and motions in 3D, allowing for the most accurate tracking possible. This application has a simple user interface with a lot of important features that are accessible to a large number of people. The process of measuring eye activities, generally known as the point of eye gaze, is called eye gaze tracking. Eye gaze technology has advanced significantly in past few years, moving away from mort accurate eye gaze trackers that required to digital video and image analysis of eye gaze movement-based in eye tracking technologies. There are some features of eye tracking system mentioned below. They are here we use a simple web camera to track eyes and detect exam cheating, there is no need for specialized hardware all that is required is a web camera and a standard PC or a laptop, for gaze tracking there is no physical contact. We can do real-time eye tracking, usability testing with eye tracking and in a single click we can operate the system. Furthermore, because no additional or complex configuration procedures are required during its setup, installing this software on your computer does not require any specific PC operating abilities. As conclusion, Gaze tracking is a simple yet reliable tool that may let record and track eye movements quickly and easily. Only thing is connect it to a supported camera device, adjust a few settings then it works.

OpenCV


OpenCV is a programming functions library aimed mostly at real-time computer vision. For image processing, I use OpenCV in my system. OpenCV is mostly used for facial detection and recognition. More than 2500 optimized algorithms are included in this library, which covers a comprehensive mix of both classic and state-of-the-art computer vision and machine learning techniques. These algorithms can be used to recognize and detect faces, identify objects, classify human behaviors in videos, track camera motions, and track moving objects and other things.
